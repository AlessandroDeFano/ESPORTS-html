Mkers, a new collaboration that brings Randstad to esports is underway

Randstad becomes official HR partner of Mkers to carry out events, training initiatives, a social communication plan and sponsorship activities: Randstad will be the main sponsor of the Valorant and Rainbow Six Siege teams, and gold sponsor of the Fifa team in all official competitions.

Randstad Italia, the first world operator in human resources services, becomes official HR partner of Mkers, the first Italian joint-stock company in the export sec ...tor. Randstad will be the main sponsor of the teams on the titles of Valorant and Rainbowsix, and gold sponsor of the Fifa team, with the logo present on the players' shirts in the official eSports competitions. As part of the partnership, Mkers and Randstad will train young students or recent graduates interested in pursuing a career in the field of export and gaming. Two six-monthly masterclasses are planned at the Mkers Gaming House, with scenario presentations, training interventions and project work related to the topics addressed. With the support of the experts of Randstad Technologies, the specialized division dedicated to the world of information technology, the goal is to create new profiles to be included in an exponentially growing sector and increasingly looking for professionals. The initiatives of Mkers and Randstad are also aimed at a business audience, such as marketing professionals, or the press. In fact, events are scheduled by the industry players, which will illustrate the characteristics and opportunities of the gaming industry. In addition to the sponsorship and training activities managed by the Randstad Sport team of consultants, the collaboration also includes a communication plan on different platforms, such as Twitch, to speak directly to the audience of passionate gamers and to Generation Z. “We are very pleased to start this new path with Mkers”, declares Simona Tansini, Ad Randstad Italy. "The partnership is based on a strong commonality of value between our realities: the world of esports, like that of traditional sports, provides daily dedication and training to improve the performance of pro-players, a common trait with the ever-evolving HR world. and which feeds on the new growth challenges within the various sectors. Approaching esports, Randstad aims at a new important sector of the market, which involves in particular the Millennials and the new generation of Generation Z. By combining our skills with Mkers' innovation, we want to invest in the formation of profiles related to the world of gaming that will become the talents that companies will need in the future ". “Another Global Leader in his sector chooses Mkers to get in direct contact with the new generations”, is the comment of Paolo Cisaria, Managing Director of Mkers. “With Randstad we will share not only the principles of innovation, passion and talent but above all the professional growth paths of the esports sector in Italy. Mkers continues to hire new resources, starting today with the support and credibility of a new Official HR Partner ".

Will Jay “Sinatraa” Won return to play on Valorant?

The suspension that led Jay “Sinatraa” Won, former Overwatch League MVP who later switched to Valorant, to no longer be able to compete in the Riot Games game is over. And now one wonders if Sinatraa, who is part of Sentinels and who had begun to show interesting skills in Valorant, will resume his business.

In March, Won was suspended after his ex-girlfriend accused him of sexual haras ...sment. At that point, Riot Games had initiated an investigation to clarify the matter and for the duration of the investigation had decided to suspend Won from any professional Valorant tournament. In May Riot Games announced that its internal investigation was over and that, in fact, it had decided to suspend Won for six months with effect from 10 March. Therefore, on 10 September last, Won's six-month suspension expired. AND NOW? - The expiry of the suspension, however, has not yet coincided with a return of Won on the professional scene. In May, on the sidelines of the announcement of his suspension, Won stated that he did not know if he would ever resume business, but that he would continue to stream his games on Valorant. In a note published on Twitter he also stressed that "I still have four months of exclusion from tournaments to try to develop a better set of interpersonal skills and regain the trust of this community". For now, Sinatraa has evaded any comment on the matter. It seems that, for now, it will remain on the sidelines of the Valorant scene.

How strong was the impact of the pandemic on esports?

In 2021, the export market trend was the protagonist of a real roller coaster ride. If on the one hand the lockdown on a global scale has forced hundreds of millions of people within the walls of their homes, enormously increasing the numbers of the viewership, on the other hand, those who had invested in certain business models found themselves having to take a hard blow all over. While even giants like football and basketball have long been for ...ced to pull the plug, for example, League of Legends has broken new records with its Worlds. But if a company like Riot Games has had the opportunity to celebrate, others like Activision-Blizzard have experienced an unprecedented crisis in their offering export. How violent has the impact of the pandemic on gaming actually been competitive? Finally we have some metrics available to be able to draw the first ones estimates. INDICATORS - Several conflicting signs have emerged over the last few months. There has been much discussion about the crisis that has characterized city-based esports franchise leagues, such as the Overwatch League and its Call of Duty equivalent from Activision-Blizzard. There has been talk of an impressive growth in the ecosystem of streaming broadcasts, so much so that the average hours of viewership dedicated to export have reached peaks never seen before. There was also talk of companies put up for sale, such as the owner of the famous Las Vegas arena Allied Esports. In short, it is extremely complicated to assess the general trend of the sector, since a sort of recession seems to have followed with the increase in visibility. But it is possible to try to clarify by studying the latest data collected by Esportearnings and The Esport Observer. The backward economy?- There are three figures that have made some insiders turn their eyebrows, such as the vice president of France Esports Nicolas Besombes. The first is in the numbers of the export tournaments held every year, which have gone from 5,654 in 2019 to just 2,428 events in 2021; it was not since 2013 that such a low figure has been recorded. The second is in the sums up for grabs in the main events, which jumped back five years: considering that in 2016 91 million dollars were invested in this way, and that growth did not stop until 257.5 million prize pools placed in 2019, it could be strange to see the sum drop to only 81 million dollars in 2021. The latest figure is in the groove that has touched the average earnings of all professionals in the sector at all levels, which are went from $ 8,300 in 2019 to $ 5,700 in 2021.
